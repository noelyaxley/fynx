# FYNX — Research Summary & Brand Strategy

## 1. Research Summary

### What the brief got right (confirmed)
- **The financing gap is real and structural.** Traditional construction loans are built around on-site milestones (slab, frame, lock-up). Prefab/modular value creation happens off-site, in factories. This is not a perception problem — it's a product design mismatch.
- **CommBank's 2025 prefab initiative is real.** They joined prefabAUS, launched assessed-manufacturer pathways (60%/80% off-site funding), and are sponsoring a standard-form contract. This validates the market but doesn't close the gap.
- **Housing supply is structurally undersupplied.** NHSAC projects a 262,000-dwelling shortfall against the Housing Accord target. 2024 completions (~177,000) were well below ~223,000 underlying demand.
- **Construction insolvency is the #1 sector for company failures** (27% of all ASIC administrations, 2023-24).
- **Prefab penetration is <5%** of Australian housing starts. Low base, high policy interest, growing government mandates.

### What the research sharpened
- **CBA is literally the only major bank with a prefab-specific product.** No other Big 4 bank has followed. Westpac, ANZ, NAB still only lend once modules are affixed. Federal Housing Minister O'Neil pressured Westpac in early 2025 — no result.
- **No non-bank lender has a prefab-specific product either.** Pepper, Liberty, La Trobe, Brighten all assess modular case-by-case through general construction products. No standardised pathway. This is genuine whitespace.
- **APRA is consulting on lower ADC (Acquisition, Development, Construction) risk weights.** This could make prefab lending more capital-efficient for banks over time — a potential tailwind.
- **The standard-form contract (CBA + prefabAUS) is not yet published.** This is the critical infrastructure piece. Once available, it lowers the barrier for other lenders.
- **State government mandates are creating demand-side pull:** QLD targets 50% MMC in government projects; NSW is mandating prefab as "building work" under BCA (2026); VIC funds regulatory parity. $174M in federal/state MMC funding.
- **prefabAUS has 180+ members** and is growing rapidly. Executive Chairman says sector is "winning nationally."
- **Market size:** AUD 14.6–16.9B (2024-25), growing at 6–11% CAGR. Projected to reach AUD 22–31B by 2028–2035.

### What to avoid or soften
- **Don't claim FYNX is a lender** unless licensing is confirmed. Use "specialist finance platform," "finance partner," "funding specialist."
- **Don't state specific rates, LVRs, or funding percentages.** The site is pre-operational; specifics create regulatory and reputational risk.
- **Don't overstate the Fabulr relationship.** Keep it clear: preferred partner, separate entity, independent governance.
- **Don't claim "first" or "only" in the market.** CBA exists. FYNX's advantage is focus, speed, and digital-native design — not being first.
- **Don't use NHSAC/ABS figures without attribution.** All stats on the site include source citations.

### Strongest positioning angles
1. **"Finance designed for the way modern homes are actually built."** — This is the line. Validated by research. CBA's existence proves the gap; FYNX fills it more completely.
2. **CBA is one product at one bank. FYNX is an entire platform.** The competitive story isn't "banks can't do this" — it's "only one bank has tried, and it's just a policy within a giant institution."
3. **Dual audience advantage.** No one else is serving both consumers and developers with modular-specific finance. Banks do consumer. Private credit does developer. FYNX does both.
4. **The standard-form contract race.** When CBA/prefabAUS publish the contract, every other lender will need it — FYNX should be ready to leverage it immediately.

## 2. Brand System

### Brand Idea
**"The Finance Layer for Modern Construction"**

FYNX is not a bank. Not a broker. Not a builder. It's the specialist finance infrastructure that sits between capital and modern construction delivery. Like a layer in a building system — essential, precise, structural.

### Positioning Statement
FYNX is Australia's specialist finance platform for prefab and modular construction. We provide funding designed for off-site manufacturing milestones, not traditional on-site draw schedules — for homeowners, investors, and developers building with modern methods.

### Value Proposition
- **For consumers:** Finance your prefab home from factory to foundation, with a specialist who understands every milestone.
- **For developers:** Capital for modular residential projects, with underwriting that speaks your delivery language.

### Message Hierarchy
1. **Primary:** Finance designed for the way modern homes are actually built.
2. **Supporting:** Specialist funding for prefab and modular construction across Australia.
3. **Proof:** Off-site milestone funding. Accredited manufacturers. Digital process.
4. **Trust:** Sydney-based. Independent. Australia-wide.

### Tonal Attributes
- Sharp, not slick
- Confident, not arrogant
- Clear, not dumbed-down
- Sophisticated, not corporate
- Australian without clichés
- Precise, not fussy

**Rationale:** Dark base avoids bank-blue cliché. Warm gold signals capital and premium without feeling old-money. The palette feels architectural, precise, and financially confident.

### Typography
| Role | Font | Weight |
|------|------|--------|
| Display / headings | Space Grotesk | 600–700 |
| Body | Inter | 400–500 |

**Rationale:** Space Grotesk is geometric and architectural — it reads as modern, precise, engineered. Inter provides perfect legibility for body copy across devices. Both are Google Fonts, zero licensing cost.

### Motion Language
- **Entrance:** Fade-up with slight Y translation (20px). Used for all content entering viewport.
- **Accent lines:** Vertical lines scale from 0 to 1 on Y axis — suggests construction, assembly, drawing.
- **Hover:** Cards translate -2px Y + border color shift to gold. Subtle, intentional.
- **Page transitions:** None (static generation = instant page loads).
- **Principle:** Motion should feel like precision assembly, not playful animation. Every movement has a structural metaphor.

### Graphic Language
- **Blueprint grid:** Thin gold/white lines on dark background (60px grid). Suggests precision, architecture, engineering drawings.
- **Module cards:** Rectangular, sharp corners, border-only on dark. Hover reveals gold accent. Each card is a "module."
- **Section labels:** Horizontal gold line + uppercase tracking label. Architectural notation style.
- **Statistics:** Large display type + gold accent + source citation. Editorial data presentation.
- **Accent lines:** Vertical gradient lines (gold to transparent). Suggests structure, columns, construction.
- **Number blocks:** Sequential numbering (01, 02, 03) in gold. Process and precision.

### Art Direction (for future assets)
- Dark, dramatic, atmospheric imagery
- Factory interiors with warm lighting
- Modular homes being craned onto sites
- Aerial views of modular developments
- Close-ups of precision joinery and CLT panels
- Australian landscapes with modern architecture
- Abstract: grid patterns, assembly sequences, architectural plans

## 3. Conversion Strategy

### Primary CTA: "Talk to FYNX"
- Appears in nav (always visible)
- Appears at bottom of every page
- Links to /contact or inline form

### Form Design
- **Segmented by audience:** Homeowner/Investor, Developer, Broker/Partner, Other
- **Low friction:** First name, last name, email required. Phone and message optional.
- **Hidden attribution fields:** page source, UTM parameters, form source
- **Success state:** Confirmation message + "within one business day" commitment

### Form Placement
- Homepage: Full form in bottom CTA section
- Product pages (prefab, developer): Full form in bottom CTA section
- Contact page: Full form as primary content
- How It Works, About, FAQ: Button CTA linking to /contact

### Ad Landing Strategy
- UTM parameters captured automatically
- Contact page works as a standalone ad landing destination
- Product pages work for audience-specific ad campaigns
- Form source field identifies which page drove the lead
