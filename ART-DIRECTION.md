# FYNX Art Direction & Asset System

## 1. Visual Audit Summary

### Current State
The FYNX site uses a dark charcoal base (#09090B) with warm gold (#D4A853) accents, Space Grotesk display + Inter body type, and a CSS blueprint grid pattern. There is one animated SVG (module assembly diagram) in the hero. Every other section is text, borders, and cards on dark backgrounds.

### What the Asset System Must Fix
- **Zero photographic or rendered imagery.** The site about financing physical buildings has no visual representation of a building, factory, crane, or home.
- **No atmospheric depth.** Every section reads as flat — same dark background, same card pattern. Needs layered backgrounds, environmental cues, and material textures.
- **No visual signature.** The site could be any dark-mode fintech. Needs a defining visual motif that says "modern construction finance" at a glance.
- **No conversion-adjacent imagery.** Forms sit in plain bordered boxes. Premium imagery near CTAs increases perceived value and trust.
- **No social/OG imagery.** The og-image.png doesn't exist.

## 2. FYNX Art Direction System

### Core Visual Idea: "Engineered Assembly"
FYNX visuals should feel like you're watching precision-engineered modules being assembled into housing — but through the lens of premium editorial photography. Not construction-site grit. Not sterile renders. Something between an architectural journal and a financial institution's annual report.

### Primary Motifs
1. **The Module** — rectangular volumes, CLT panels, steel connections. The fundamental unit of modular construction, treated as a design object.
2. **The Grid** — blueprint precision lines, connection points, structural frameworks. Suggests engineering, planning, capital allocation.
3. **The Crane Moment** — a module being lowered into place. The defining moment of modular construction. FYNX's visual equivalent of a ribbon-cutting.
4. **The Factory Floor** — controlled, lit, orderly. Where value is actually created. Anti-thesis of chaotic construction sites.
5. **The Australian Context** — golden light, eucalyptus landscape, coastal light, suburban texture. Not generic international imagery.

### Palette Logic
All imagery must work against the dark site background:
- **Primary ground:** Near-black (#09090B to #111114) — images should have deep shadows and dark zones that bleed into the site
- **Gold accent light:** Warm amber/gold (#D4A853) should appear as natural lighting within scenes — late afternoon sun, factory tungsten, golden hour
- **Cool structural tones:** Steel blue-greys, concrete neutrals, matte charcoal — the materials of modern construction
- **Selective warmth:** CLT timber warmth, interior glow — signals the human/residential outcome

### Materials (what should be visible in images)
- Cross-laminated timber (CLT) — warm, textured, engineered
- Steel connections and brackets — precision, structural integrity
- Matte concrete — foundations, stability
- Glass — reflections, transparency, modernity
- Brushed metal — premium hardware, connections
- Australian landscape — dry grass, eucalyptus, coastal light

### Lighting Rules
- **Primary:** Warm directional light from a single source — golden hour or factory tungsten. Creates dramatic shadows.
- **Secondary:** Cool ambient fill — prevents crush, adds dimension
- **Accent:** Edge/rim light in gold catching metal surfaces or timber grain
- **Avoid:** Flat, even studio lighting. Overcast flat grey. Flash photography look.

### Composition Rules
- **Spacious** — generous negative space, room to breathe. Not crowded.
- **Off-center subject** — rule of thirds or golden ratio. Leaves room for text overlay.
- **Strong horizontals and verticals** — echoing the grid/module motif. Avoid diagonals unless deliberate.
- **Depth layers** — foreground detail, mid-ground subject, background context. Creates cinematic feel.
- **Low camera angles** — looking up at structures suggests authority, scale. Good for hero/developer imagery.

### Do-Not-Use List
- Stock photos of people in hard hats smiling at cameras
- Aerial drone shots of generic suburban housing estates
- Blue-tinted "tech" imagery
- Generic city skyline shots
- Handshake/deal imagery
- Clip-art style construction icons
- White background product-on-marble shots
- Overly rendered/CGI architecture
- Green eco-branding leaf motifs

### How Finance and Construction Intersect Visually
Finance is invisible — you can't photograph capital. Construction is physical — you can photograph modules, factories, cranes. The visual intersection happens through:
- **Precision** — the obsessive alignment of a CLT panel echoes the precision of structured finance
- **Staging** — factory production lines mirror milestone-based funding stages
- **Assembly** — modules coming together mirrors capital stack structuring
- **Completion** — the finished home is the outcome of both building and financing

## 4. Nana Banana Prompt Library

### HERO FAMILY

#### H1 — Brand Signature: "The Crane Moment"
**Page:** Homepage hero background
**Purpose:** Defining brand image — the moment a modular home module is lowered into place
**Ratio:** 16:9
**Prompt:**
A cinematic wide shot of a prefabricated home module being lowered by crane onto a prepared concrete foundation at golden hour in rural Australia. The module is a rectangular timber-clad volume, suspended by steel cables, hovering one metre above connection points on the slab. Warm late afternoon Australian sunlight catches the brushed steel crane cables and the cross-laminated timber facade of the module. The surrounding landscape is dry native Australian scrubland with scattered eucalyptus trees. The sky is deep amber to charcoal gradient. Shot from a low angle looking up at the module against the sky, creating a sense of scale and precision. The composition leaves generous empty space in the upper left quadrant for text overlay. Captured with a Sony A7R IV, 24mm wide angle lens at f/8, golden hour directional lighting from camera right. The mood is dramatic, precise, and purposeful — like watching the final piece of a system click into place. Editorial architectural photography with cinematic depth and premium commercial polish. Restrained warm palette of deep charcoal, amber gold, timber honey, and steel grey.
**Avoid:** Construction site mess, visible workers, safety vests, overcast sky, suburban context, bright daylight
**Post-processing:** Darken shadows significantly to blend with #09090B site background. Use at 8-12% opacity as hero background.

#### H2 — Factory Interior: "Where Value Is Created"
**Page:** Homepage Problem section background, Prefab page
**Purpose:** Establish that modular homes are precision-manufactured, not improvised
**Ratio:** 16:9
**Prompt:**
Interior of a modern prefabricated home manufacturing facility. A long production line stretches into the distance under warm amber industrial pendant lights. Cross-laminated timber panels are arranged in orderly stacks along the left side. In the centre, a partially assembled residential module sits on a steel production frame — walls up, ceiling panels being positioned. The factory floor is clean polished concrete, immaculately organised. Precision robotic positioning arms are visible in the mid-ground. The perspective is a strong vanishing point down the production line, creating depth and rhythm. Warm tungsten lighting from above creates dramatic pools of light with deep shadows between workstations. Shot from eye level with a Canon EOS R5, 35mm lens at f/4, creating moderate depth of field. The mood is controlled, engineered, and purposeful — a manufacturing environment that produces homes with factory-grade precision. ## 5. Remotion Motion Plan

### R1 — Hero: Module Assembly Sequence (REPLACE current SVG)
**Location:** Homepage hero, right column
**Purpose:** Animate the defining brand moment — a module dropping into place
**What it communicates:** Precision, assembly, the FYNX funding process as a parallel to physical construction
**Duration:** 8 seconds, loops seamlessly
**Style:** Dark background with gold/amber wireframe lines. Modules as outlined rectangular volumes. Blueprint annotation style. Not photorealistic — architectural diagram in motion.
**Implementation:** Remotion composition → rendered as MP4 with alpha → `<video>` element with autoplay loop muted playsInline. Still fallback for mobile/reduced-motion using a single frame export.
**Sequence:**
1. Grid lines draw themselves (0-2s)
2. Foundation rectangle appears (2-3s)
3. Module A slides in from left, settles onto foundation (3-5s)
4. Module B slides in from right, connects (5-6.5s)
5. Roof line draws itself (6.5-7.5s)
6. Connection points flash gold (7.5-8s)
7. Hold complete form briefly, then fade to restart

**Location:** How It Works milestone grid section
**Purpose:** Animated visual showing funding flowing through milestones
**What it communicates:** Continuous progress, each stage triggering the next
**Duration:** 12 seconds, loops
**Style:** Horizontal progress line with milestone nodes. Gold line fills left-to-right through each stage. Node labels appear as the line reaches them. Same palette as site.
**Implementation:** Remotion → rendered as MP4. Or implemented as CSS/Framer Motion animation triggered on scroll. The Remotion version would be higher quality.

### R3 — Blueprint Line Draw (Background Texture)
**Location:** Any section using grid-bg class
**Purpose:** Subtle ambient animation — grid lines slowly drawing and fading

## 6. Implementation Notes

### Image Integration Approach
1. Generate images at 2K resolution minimum (4K for hero candidates)
2. Post-process: darken heavily, match to site palette
3. Place as `<Image>` components with `priority` for above-fold, `loading="lazy"` for below
4. Use `object-fit: cover` with `opacity: 0.08` to `0.12` for background images
5. Always provide the section's content as the readable foreground — images are atmosphere, not content
6. Use `<picture>` with WebP for performance, PNG fallback
7. All images should have empty `alt=""` when decorative background, descriptive alt when inline

### Directory Structure
```
/public/images/
  hero/
    crane-moment.webp      (H1)
    crane-moment-mobile.webp
  sections/
    factory-interior.webp   (H2)
    completed-home.webp     (H3)
    townhouse-dev.webp      (H4)
  details/
    precision-connection.webp (D1)
    blueprint-drawing.webp    (D2)
  textures/
    abstract-grid.webp      (T1)
    clt-timber.webp         (T2)
  social/
    og-image.png            (S1)
  campaign/
    ads-hero-square.webp    (C1)
```

### Responsive Strategy
- Hero backgrounds: serve different crops for mobile (4:3 crop) vs desktop (16:9)
- Use `next/image` with `sizes` prop for automatic responsive serving
- Mobile: reduce opacity of background images by additional 30-50% for readability
- `prefers-reduced-motion`: replace videos with static poster frames
